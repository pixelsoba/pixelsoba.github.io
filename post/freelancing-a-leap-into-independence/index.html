Freelancing: A Leap into Independence

May 27, 2021 · Augustin Ambiehl

May 27, 2021

This date celebrates three things: my 26th birthday, the day I resigned, and the beginning of this blog.

The need to move forward

I’ve always had this need to progress, even without a concrete objective, but knowing that I’m on a path where I’m making progress. After my BAC (high school diploma), I looked at all my friends who didn’t want to continue studying and went straight into work with some confusion. The same thing happened after my IUT and bachelor’s degree: many of my friends didn’t want to pursue their studies further. Now that I’m more mature, I understand that not everyone dreams of long studies, complex jobs, or roles with heavy responsibilities.

In 2018, the choice of company for my final year internship was carefully considered. With a background in both biology and computer science, I was looking for a software development company offering real technical challenges to push me out of my comfort zone and allow me to learn as much as possible. I was lucky enough to find Diginext, which perfectly met my expectations in this regard, and especially which offered me a full-time contract at the end of my internship.

After 3 years at Diginext, I settled in well, learned a lot about the business world, and teamwork. Most importantly, I sharpened my technical skills. Despite pretty good relationships with colleagues, a few things weren’t going well. There was a growing feeling of stagnation and weariness, compounded by a lack of effective management and oversight —both in terms of project guidance and financial compensation. This led to more questioning: what will my future look like if I stay here? Maybe I will get a promotion. Maybe I will gain more responsibilities. But what’s the point? The idea of waking up one morning and realizing that I’ve been working at the same place for 15 years is distressing. At that moment, I was only sure of one thing: I must leave.

So I quit.

The start of something new

Here I am in the jungle with the only plan of not being an employee: if I don’t work on my projects, at least I’ll be my own boss. At this point, I haven’t made any final decisions about my future mission; I do have a few options, but I still have to choose. I’ve realized that shorter, more focused missions align best with my working style, allowing me to deliver better energy and thus results. I also expect to have more time for my personal projects (stepping up on the Godot Engine and taking on renovation work), while ultimately aiming for better financial compensation.

Best of luck in your projects●
